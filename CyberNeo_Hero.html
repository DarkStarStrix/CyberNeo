Why Cyber NEO Exists

The modern dev environment is bloated, slow, and full of spyware. IDEs look like shopping malls. Operating systems spy on you more than they serve you. Every keystroke feels like fighting through mud.

Cyber NEO is the opposite.

  • Built on Arch Linux (lean, fast, endlessly customizable).
  • Runs Hyperland with a cyberpunk tiling workflow.
  • Ships with only the sharpest tools: NeoVim (LazyVim) and Zed.
  • Optimized for keyboard-first workflows (ortholinear-ready).
  • Ready out-of-the-box for AI engineering, SWE, and research.

This isn’t a “general audience” distro. It’s a blade. It’s dangerous. But in the right hands? You move at the speed of thought.

FAQ

Q: What is CyberNEO?
A: CyberNEO is a custom Arch-based Linux distribution designed for developers, ML engineers, and hackers who want a fast, minimal, and stylish OS. It’s built around Hyperland for tiling, NeoVim/Zed for editing, and a carefully curated set of tools for both productivity and entertainment.
Q: Who is CyberNEO for?
A: CyberNEO is for people who want control over their environment. If you’re the type who prefers lean tools, fast performance, and minimal bloat over convenience, this OS is designed for you.
Q: Why not just use stock Arch or another distro?
A: You could. CyberNEO simply saves you the time of ricing and configuring everything yourself. It bakes in a strong set of defaults and design choices so you can dive right in without compromise.
Q: Does it come with GPU/ML support out of the box?
A: Yes. CyberNEO ships with NVIDIA and ROCm drivers pre-configured, along with ML tooling managed through uv. No more dependency chaos with pip, conda, or poetry.
Q: Which editors are included?
A: Only two: NeoVim (with LazyVim as a modern config layer) and Zed. No VS Code, no JetBrains. If you want more, you’re free to add them — but this distro has opinions.
Q: Can I use CyberNEO for everyday tasks?
A: Absolutely. Beyond coding and ML, CyberNEO also comes with media tools: manga readers, video players, and lightweight utilities. It’s designed as both a development environment and a daily driver.
Q: What makes it “cyber”?
A: The aesthetic. Neon-themed styling, terminal-forward workflows, and a hacker-inspired vibe. CyberNEO isn’t trying to look corporate; it’s designed to feel like your personal command center.
Q: How stable is it?
A: CyberNEO is based on Arch, so it follows a rolling release model. It’s not as “stable” as Debian or Ubuntu LTS, but that’s the price of living on the edge. Rolling updates mean you’ll always be bleeding-edge, and rollbacks are possible if something breaks.
Q: Can I contribute?
A: Yes. Config files, package suggestions, bug reports, and rice ideas are welcome. CyberNEO is meant to evolve as a community-driven, opinionated distro.
